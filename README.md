PHYS_1655 final project 

This dataset contains medical insurance information for 1,338 individuals. The data includes demographic and health-related variables such as age, sex, BMI, number of children, smoking status, region, and medical charges. After analyzing the data, we can see that the insurance costs are highly skewed, most individuals have relatively low charges, but a small group incurs very high expenses. The most significant finding is that smoking status has a strong impact on medical costs, smokers have substantially higher average insurance charges than non-smokers. While factors like age and BMI contribute to variation in charges, they do not explain the differences as strongly as smoking does. This indicates that smoking is a major driver of increased healthcare expenses in this dataset.
